Ayurveda is an ancient system of natural healing that originated in India over 5,000 years ago. It focuses on achieving balance in the body through diet, lifestyle, and natural remedies. The word "Ayurveda" means "knowledge of life" in Sanskrit.

ğŸ“œ Core Principles of Ayurveda

  • Balance of the five elements: earth, water, fire, air, and space
  • The concept of three doshas: Vata, Pitta, and Kapha
  • Use of herbs, minerals, and massage therapies
  • Daily routines (Dinacharya) and seasonal practices (Ritucharya)

ğŸ”º Types of Doshas & Their Features

Vata

  • Elements: Air & Space
  • Qualities: Light, dry, mobile, cold
  • Controls: Movement, breathing, circulation

Pitta

  • Elements: Fire & Water
  • Qualities: Hot, sharp, oily
  • Controls: Digestion, metabolism, energy

Kapha

  • Elements: Earth & Water
  • Qualities: Heavy, slow, steady, cold
  • Controls: Structure, immunity, lubrication

ğŸ“š History and Origin of Ayurveda

Ayurveda has its roots in the Vedic texts of ancient India. It was practiced and passed down by sages and healers. The knowledge was systematically compiled in texts like Charaka Samhita and Sushruta Samhita. Ayurveda emphasizes preventive care and holistic healing.
